Current Water Temperature versus Historical Climatology

Current daily average water temperatures (grey) plotted against the long term climatology (green) with the second Standard Deviations (SD) (orange and light blue) and the third SD limits (red and blue) shown. Temperatures are considered to be 'normal' if they lie between the +/-2 SD lines, temperatures between the +/-2 and +/-3 SD lines are considered 'warmer' or 'cooler' and temperatures outside the +/- 3 SD limits are 'much warmer' or 'much cooler' than the long term climatology.

Day and Night Water Temperature Anomaly

Temperature anomaly - the difference between the measured water temperature and the long term average. The grey line is the anomaly value, orange and light blue are two Standard Deviations (SD) from the long term mean and red and blue are three SD from the long term mean.

These groupings can be classed by the following terminology:

  • Normal (within 2 SD of long-term mean)
  • Warmer (more than 2 SD warmer than long-term mean)
  • Cooler (more than 2 SD cooler than long-term mean)
  • Much Warmer (more than 3 SD warmer than long-term mean)
  • Much Cooler (more than 3 SD cooler than long-term mean)
