GBR Bleaching Watch

This site shows current water temperatures for selected reefs along the Great Barrier Reef using real time data from the AIMS and IMOS Remote Monitoring stations. Data is updated everyday.

The data is displayed in the context of the historical climatological record (how much warmer or cooler it is than the historical climatology) and how current temperatures relate to known empirical coral bleaching thresholds.


Data that is within +/- 2 Standard Deviations (SD) from the long term mean is considered to be 'normal', data between the +/- 2 and 3 SD limits as 'unusual' and temperatures outside the +/- 3 SD as 'extreme'.
